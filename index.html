Construction Types Explained

A beginner-friendly visual guide to help you understand building classifications used in underwriting.

Frame

Wood-framed buildings. Common for single or multi-family homes in suburban or residential areas. Cost-efficient, but highly combustible and less durable in fires.

Joisted Masonry (JM)

Brick or block exterior walls with wood framing inside. Often used in older commercial buildings and small apartment complexes. Moderate fire risk due to wooden joists.

Non-Combustible

Built entirely with non-combustible materials like steel or concrete. Common in industrial buildings and retail centers. Better fire resistance and lower insurance risk.

Masonry Non-Combustible (MNC)

Brick or block walls and non-combustible floors and roof. Frequently found in commercial structures and warehouses. Good fire performance and structural integrity.

Modified Fire Resistive

Specialized fire-rated construction that withstands fire for 1–2 hours. Seen in hospitals, government buildings, and mid-rise offices. High safety but higher cost.
